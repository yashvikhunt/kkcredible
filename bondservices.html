What are tax-free bonds?

The tax-free bonds normally provide low interest-rates and high tenors as compared to the taxable bonds. It defined as the most suitable means of financing planning for eligible investors. High-income earners generally prefer the tax-free bonds.

These bonds are mainly issued by municipal, state governments or county and interest payment for these types of bonds exempt from federal income tax and in some instances, local/state income tax. The interest rate relating to terms of bonds along with credit quality is lower as compared to taxable bonds. The eligible investors for tax-free bonds include:

  • Nonprofit healthcare.
  • Nonprofit schools.
  • Nonprofit higher education.
  • Nonprofit institutions, etc.
Types of tax-free bonds

There are mainly two types of tax-free bonds, they are:

  • General obligation bonds (GO):

    These are the bonds that are issued by a state, county, or city usually not secured by any assets. The GO bonds backed by the ‘full faith and credit’ of the issuer having the capability to tax residents in order to pay bondholders.

  • Revenue bonds:

    These are the type of bonds that are not backed by the government’s taxing power; however, revenues from a definite project like lease fees, highway tolls, and electric power plants. The revenue bonds generally have higher interest rates than that of GO bonds.
